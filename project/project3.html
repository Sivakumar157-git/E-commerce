Project Overview

Home Garden
2–4 weeks · Beginner
Discover how to create and nurture a sustainable home garden, growing fresh vegetables, herbs, and flowers that enhance your ecological footprint. Home gardening is the practice of growing vegetables, herbs, flowers, and small fruits in the available space around your home, such as balconies, terraces, backyards, or even windowsills. It allows you to produce fresh, chemical-free food while improving your environment and overall well-being. A good home garden uses healthy organic soil, receives at least four to six hours of sunlight, and follows simple care routines like regular watering, occasional composting, and natural pest control. It also creates a relaxing atmosphere, supports biodiversity by attracting pollinators, and reduces household costs by providing daily-use vegetables and herbs. With basic planning and consistent care, even small spaces can become productive and sustainable green areas.
Energy Audit
1 week · Intermediate
Learn to conduct an energy audit for your home or workplace and identify opportunities for reducing energy consumption and saving costs. An energy audit is a systematic assessment of how energy is used in a home, office, or workplace to identify areas where consumption can be reduced without affecting comfort or productivity. It involves analyzing electricity bills, inspecting appliances and lighting, evaluating insulation and ventilation, and checking for energy leaks or inefficiencies in major systems such as HVAC, refrigeration, and motors. By understanding how and where energy is wasted, an audit helps you implement practical improvements like adopting efficient appliances, upgrading lighting to LEDs, optimizing thermostat settings, sealing air gaps, or introducing renewable energy options. This process not only lowers electricity bills but also reduces carbon footprint and improves overall energy efficiency, making it a valuable skill for sustainable living and cost-effective facility management.
Zero Waste Challenge
1 week · Advanced
Take part in a challenge to minimize daily waste and adopt sustainable habits for a healthier planet. It encourages you to evaluate your daily routines, identify common sources of waste such as single-use plastics and unnecessary packaging, and replace them with reusable, recyclable, or compostable alternatives. The challenge also promotes responsible consumption by emphasizing thoughtful buying decisions, repurposing items instead of discarding them, and managing organic waste through composting. By tracking your waste output and making small but consistent changes—like carrying reusable bags, minimizing food waste, choosing eco-friendly products, and recycling effectively—you build long-term habits that lower your environmental impact and inspire a more sustainable lifestyle.
